About the Seminar

Korea's ICT industry has achieved remarkable growth through government policy support and technological innovation. Now, leveraging this success, Korea aims to establish collaborative models with developing countries for mutual growth. The conference will feature ICT convergence business models prepared by 190 officials from 52 countries who graduated from or are enrolled in KOICA-supported master's programs, alongside presentations from leading Korean ICT companies.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I register for the seminar?

Registration will be available on-site on the day of the event. Please arrive early to ensure your spot as seating may be limited.

Is there a registration fee?

The seminar is completely free for all registered participants. This includes access to all sessions, networking opportunities, and refreshments.

Will presentation materials be available?

Yes, presentation slides and materials will be made available to registered participants after the event through our official channels.

Is parking available at the venue?

Limited parking is available on campus. We recommend using public transportation as the venue is easily accessible via subway and bus.
